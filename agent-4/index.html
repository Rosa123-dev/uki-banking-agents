What You're Building

What Your Client Relationship Agent Can Do

This agent connects your day-to-day client interactions with Accenture's commercial intelligence — helping you spot the right moment to bring the right insight to the right client.

Relationship Health Check

Scan recent emails and meetings with a specific client. Summarise the state of the relationship — tone, frequency, open actions, and any signals of risk or opportunity.

Opportunity Spotting

Read a client email thread and identify commercial signals — budget mentions, strategic challenges, technology pain points — and suggest what Accenture services could address them.

Thought Leadership Matching

Given a client's current challenges or interests, search SharePoint for relevant internal assets and the web for Accenture.com publications — then summarise and suggest how to use them.

Meeting Preparation

Before any client meeting, get a full brief: relationship history, open topics, relevant news about their organisation, and 2–3 smart commercial questions to ask.

Market & News Intelligence

Search for recent news about a client's organisation, their sector, or regulatory developments affecting them — and suggest timely, relevant talking points.

Touchpoint Suggestions

Recommend when and how to reach out — seasonal greetings, relevant industry events, publication anniversaries, or simply flagging when a client has gone quiet.

How It Works

From Client Signal to Commercial Action

The agent connects three sources of intelligence — your live M365 data, your internal SharePoint assets, and the public web — and turns them into actionable commercial insights.

1

Read the Signal

Scans emails, meeting notes, and Teams chats for client mentions, challenges, priorities, and commercial hints.

2

Search Internally

Searches SharePoint for relevant Accenture thought leadership, proposals, case studies, and assets saved to your team site.

3

Search Externally

Uses web search to find recent Accenture.com publications, industry news, and client organisation updates relevant to the conversation.

4

Suggest the Action

Produces a prioritised list of recommended next steps — from sharing a specific paper to drafting an outreach email or flagging an opportunity to pursue.

Internal Sources (M365)

  • Outlook emails with and about the client
  • Calendar meetings and any attached briefing docs
  • Teams messages and channel conversations
  • SharePoint: saved thought leadership, proposals, account plans
  • OneNote meeting notes if stored in Teams

External Sources (Web Search)

  • Accenture.com thought leadership and research reports
  • Client organisation news, press releases, and results
  • Industry and regulatory developments in banking
  • Competitor moves and market context
  • Relevant events, awards, or anniversaries

The Most Important Part

Writing Your Agent's Instructions

This agent is more sophisticated than Agent 1 — it needs to understand commercial context, know how to search intelligently, and produce output that is genuinely actionable for a client-facing professional.

✅ What a strong prompt includes

  • Your name, role, and the portfolio of clients you manage
  • How to categorise opportunity signals (e.g. by Accenture service line)
  • Which SharePoint locations hold your thought leadership assets
  • Instructions to always search the web for latest Accenture.com content
  • A clear output format for opportunity briefs and touchpoint suggestions
  • Rules for tone — commercial and precise, never pushy or generic
  • How to flag relationship risk (e.g. client gone quiet for 30+ days)
  • Seasonal and calendar awareness — quarter end, budget cycles, events
  • What it should never do — e.g. never suggest reaching out without context

❌ Common mistakes to avoid

  • Not telling it which clients to focus on — it needs a portfolio to work with
  • Forgetting to mention Accenture.com as an explicit search source
  • Not specifying output structure — you'll get paragraphs instead of action lists
  • Leaving out commercial framing — it won't know what an "opportunity" means to you
  • No guidance on tone — client outreach drafts can easily sound generic
  • Not telling it how to prioritise — high-value clients vs. smaller accounts
  • Skipping the "what not to do" rules — guardrails matter for client comms
💡 Example Prompt — Copy, Edit & Make It Yours
Full System Prompt Example

You are an intelligent client relationship assistant for [YOUR NAME], a [YOUR ROLE] at Accenture working with UKI Banking clients. Your purpose is to help me identify commercial opportunities, match Accenture thought leadership to client needs, and maintain strong, proactive client relationships. MY CLIENT PORTFOLIO: [List your key clients here — e.g. "Barclays (strategic account), Lloyds Banking Group (growth account), Metro Bank (developing account)"] YOUR THREE CORE JOBS: 1. OPPORTUNITY SPOTTING When I share an email thread, meeting summary, or client update with you: - Extract any signals that suggest a client challenge, strategic priority, technology pain point, or budget/decision moment - Map each signal to a relevant Accenture service area (e.g. Technology Transformation, AI & Data, Risk & Compliance, Cost Reduction, Cloud Migration) - Rate each opportunity: High / Medium / Low based on urgency and commercial relevance - Suggest a recommended next action for each (e.g. "Share the Banking on AI report", "Request an exploratory conversation", "Loop in [service line] SME") Output format: | Signal | Service Area | Opportunity Rating | Recommended Action | 2. THOUGHT LEADERSHIP MATCHING When asked to find relevant content for a client: - First search my SharePoint for saved Accenture assets relevant to the client's challenges - Then search the web specifically on accenture.com for the latest published thought leadership, research reports, and point of views - Summarise each piece in 2 sentences: what it covers and why it's relevant to this client - Always include the URL or SharePoint link where available - Prioritise recency — content published in the last 12 months ranks higher Output format: | Title | Source | Published | Why Relevant | Suggested Use | 3. RELATIONSHIP MANAGEMENT & TOUCHPOINTS Proactively suggest reasons to reach out that feel natural and valuable — not forced: - Flag if a client has not been contacted in 30+ days - Suggest seasonal touchpoints: quarter-end check-ins, budget season conversations, year-end messages - Surface relevant industry news, regulatory changes, or market events that give a genuine reason to reach out - Suggest relevant Accenture events, webinars, or roundtables the client might value - Draft outreach messages when asked — warm, professional, with a clear reason to connect TONE RULES: - Always commercial and precise. Never generic or salesy. - Outreach drafts should feel like they come from a trusted advisor, not a sales rep. - Never suggest reaching out without a specific, relevant reason. - Keep opportunity briefs concise — 3 bullet points maximum per opportunity. SEARCH BEHAVIOUR: - Always search accenture.com when asked for thought leadership — do not rely on training data alone. - When searching for client news, include their organisation name + "banking" + current year. - Prioritise sources: accenture.com > FT/Reuters/BBC > industry publications > general web. At the end of any analysis, always ask: "Would you like me to draft an outreach email, find more content, or add this to your opportunity tracker?"

🚀 Ways to Take It Further
01

Build a client opportunity tracker

Ask the agent to maintain a running log of opportunities spotted across all your clients — updated each time you share new client communications with it.

02

Create a weekly relationship digest

Every Monday morning, ask it to review the past week's client interactions and produce a digest: relationship health per client, open actions, and suggested touchpoints for the week ahead.

03

Link it to your CRM language

Tell the agent to use your organisation's CRM terminology (e.g. pipeline stages, opportunity types) so its output slots directly into your Salesforce or Microsoft Dynamics records.
