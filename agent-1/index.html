What Your Secretary Can Do

Because your agent is built inside Microsoft 365 Copilot, it has secure access to your real work data — emails, meetings, chats and files. Here's what you can make it do.

Email Triage

Get a prioritised summary of unread emails, flag urgent items, and surface anything that needs a reply today.

Meeting Preparation

Before any meeting, get a briefing — who's attending, what was discussed last time, relevant emails and files.

Teams Catch-Up

Summarise what you missed in Teams channels and chats while you were away or in back-to-back meetings.

Draft Replies

Ask it to draft a response to any email in your tone — then review and send. You stay in control.

Find Anything

Search across emails, files and SharePoint for that document or conversation you can never quite locate.

Summarise Documents

Drop a SharePoint link and ask for a summary, key actions, or a comparison against another document.

The Most Important Part

Writing Your Agent's Instructions

The instructions (also called a system prompt) are what make your agent yours. This is where you tell it who it is, how it should behave, and what it should focus on. Don't skip this — a great prompt makes the difference between a generic assistant and one that actually works for you.

✅ What a strong prompt includes

  • A clear role — "You are my personal work assistant"
  • Your name and job context so it personalises responses
  • What it should prioritise (e.g. emails from specific people)
  • The tone you want — formal, concise, friendly
  • What it should always do (e.g. end every brief with action items)
  • What it should never do (e.g. send emails without asking me first)
  • How you want information structured (bullet points, summaries etc.)

❌ Common mistakes to avoid

  • Being too vague — "be helpful" tells it nothing specific
  • Forgetting to set boundaries on what it can act on
  • Not specifying a tone — it'll default to generic corporate speak
  • Writing one long paragraph — use sections or bullet points
  • Skipping context about your role — it won't know what matters to you
  • Leaving out formatting preferences — you'll get walls of text
💡 Example Prompt — Copy, Edit & Make It Yours
Full System Prompt Example

You are my personal AI work secretary. My name is [YOUR NAME] and I work as a [YOUR ROLE] at Accenture, focused on [YOUR AREA e.g. UKI Banking clients]. Your job is to help me stay on top of my day. You have access to my Outlook emails, my calendar, my Teams messages, and my SharePoint files. Use all of these to give me accurate, relevant, up-to-date answers. When I ask for a daily briefing, always structure it as: 1. Urgent emails that need a reply today 2. Meetings today — with a one-line summary of what each is about 3. Any important Teams messages I've missed 4. One thing I should be aware of or follow up on When preparing me for a meeting: - Tell me who is attending and their role - Summarise any relevant recent email threads with those people - Pull out any documents shared in relation to this meeting - Suggest 2–3 smart questions I could ask When drafting emails for me: - Match my tone: professional but warm, concise, no waffle - Always show me the draft before sending — never send automatically - Keep subject lines short and clear Always respond in bullet points or short paragraphs. Never give me walls of text. If you're unsure about something, say so — don't guess.

🧪 Test Prompts — Try These Once Your Agent Is Built
Morning Briefing

"Give me my morning briefing — what emails need attention today and what meetings do I have?"

Meeting Prep

"Prepare me for my 2pm meeting — who's attending, what's the context, and what should I know going in?"

Email Triage

"Which emails in my inbox have been waiting more than 2 days for a reply from me?"

Draft a Reply

"Draft a polite reply to [person]'s last email saying I'll have the document ready by Friday."

Teams Catch-Up

"What did I miss in my Teams channels while I was in meetings this afternoon?"

Find Something

"Find the latest version of the Q2 client proposal and give me a one-paragraph summary."

🚀 Ways to Take It Further
01

Make it time-aware

Tell it to always check the date before responding so its briefings reflect what's actually coming up, not what's already passed.

02

Add VIP rules

Instruct it to always flag emails from specific people (your manager, key clients) as high priority regardless of subject.

03

Give it your writing style

Paste in an example of an email you've written and say "match this tone when drafting replies for me."
